How to Make Tandoori chicken

Tandoori chicken is a visually stunning and flavorful dish that captures the essence of Indian culinary tradition. The chicken pieces, bathed in a vibrant red marinade, are cooked to succulent perfection in a tandoor, a cylindrical clay oven. This unique cooking method imbues the chicken with a smoky aroma and a beautifully charred exterior. The key to tandoori chicken lies in the marinade, a symphony of aromatic spices like yogurt, ginger, garlic, garam masala, and red chili powder. These ingredients not only tenderize the chicken but also infuse it with a complex depth of flavor. The addition of red food coloring is optional, but it gives the dish its signature reddish hue.

The presentation of tandoori chicken is a feast for the eyes even before the first bite. The vibrantly colored chicken pieces are often served on a bed of aromatic rice or alongside cooling raita, a yogurt-based dip with cucumber or mint. Each bite bursts with flavor - the juicy and tender chicken complements the smoky char, while the symphony of spices dances on your palate. The contrasting textures, from the crisp exterior to the succulent interior, add another layer of delight to the experience. Tandoori chicken is a dish that transcends its origins, enjoyed not just in India but around the world, offering a taste of the vibrant and flavorful world of Indian cuisine.

Ingredients :

  • Yogurt: The base for tenderizing and adding tang (1 cup plain yogurt).
  • Lemon Juice: Adds acidity and brightness (1 tablespoon).
  • Ginger-Garlic Paste: For an aromatic base (1 tablespoon).
  • Garam Masala: A blend of warm spices (1 tablespoon).
  • Red Chili Powder: Adjust for your spice preference (1-2 teaspoons).
  • Cumin Powder: For an earthy flavor (1 teaspoon).
  • Coriander Powder: For a warm and citrusy aroma (1 teaspoon).
  • Turmeric Powder: Adds color and a subtle earthy flavor (½ teaspoon).
  • Salt: To taste.
  • Red Food Coloring: For a vibrant red hue (a pinch).
  • Chicken Pieces: Bone-in or boneless, skinless chicken thighs or legs (about 1 ½ - 2 lbs).
  • Vegetable Oil: For coating the chicken before marinating (1 tablespoon).
  • Lemon Wedges: For squeezing over the chicken while serving.
  • Chopped Fresh Cilantro: For garnish.

Instructions :

  • In a large bowl, whisk together the yogurt, lemon juice, ginger-garlic paste, garam masala, red chili powder (adjust for your spice preference), cumin powder, coriander powder, turmeric powder, and salt. (Optional) If using, stir in a pinch of red food coloring for a vibrant red hue.
  • Pat the chicken pieces dry with paper towels. This helps the marinade adhere better.
  • Lightly brush or coat the chicken pieces with vegetable oil.
  • Add the chicken pieces to the marinade and toss to coat them evenly.
  • Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and marinate the chicken in the refrigerator for at least 4 hours, or ideally overnight, for the best flavor.**
  • There are two main ways to cook tandoori chicken: using a tandoor oven or using your oven.
  • Preheat your tandoor oven to high heat (around 480°C or 900°F).
  • Thread the marinated chicken pieces onto skewers. You can soak the skewers in water for 30 minutes beforehand to prevent burning.
  • Carefully place the skewers in the tandoor oven and cook for 10-15 minutes per side, or until the chicken is cooked through and nicely charred.Using a Regular Oven:
  • Preheat your oven to 470°F (240°C).
  • Line a baking sheet with aluminum foil for easy cleanup. You can also place a wire rack over the baking sheet for better air circulation.
  • Arrange the marinated chicken pieces on the prepared baking sheet.
  • Broil the chicken for 10-15 minutes per side, or until the chicken is cooked through and nicely charred. Keep an eye on the chicken under the broiler to prevent burning.
  • Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C) with the grill function on.
  • Place the marinated chicken pieces on a wire rack set over a baking sheet.
  • Grill the chicken for 20-25 minutes per side, or until the chicken is cooked through and nicely charred.
  • Once cooked, transfer the tandoori chicken to a serving plate.
The presentation of tandoori chicken elevates it from a simple dish to a visually stunning centerpiece. The vibrant red chicken pieces, glistening with marinade and boasting a beautiful char, are often arranged on a bed of fluffy white rice. This bed of rice not only provides a textural contrast but also acts as a blank canvas to showcase the colorful chicken. A dollop of cooling raita, a yogurt-based dip with cucumber or mint, sits beside the chicken, offering a refreshing counterpoint to the smoky spice. Fresh cilantro adds a pop of green and a burst of herbal aroma. Tandoor ovens traditionally impart a unique smoky flavor, but even when cooked in a regular oven, the charring adds another layer of complexity.

Tandoori chicken is perfect for a celebratory meal or a casual gathering. The vibrant presentation and the enticing aroma make it a showstopper, sure to impress your guests. The ease of preparation, where the chicken marinates while you go about your day, allows for effortless hosting. Whether enjoyed as part of a festive meal or a relaxed dinner with friends, tandoori chicken transcends time and occasion. It's a versatile dish that embodies the essence of Indian cuisine - flavorful, visually appealing, and perfect for sharing with loved ones.
