How to Make Laab

Laab, also known as Larb, is a vibrant and dynamic Thai salad that explodes with bold flavors and textures. Unlike its wetter salad counterparts, laab takes pride in its distinct dryish texture. Ground meat, typically pork or chicken, forms the base, but laab can also be enjoyed vegetarian-style with crumbled tofu. The key to laab's success lies in its unique dressing. Fish sauce adds a savory depth, while lime juice provides a refreshing tang. Toasted rice powder, a signature ingredient, infuses the dish with a nutty aroma and a delightful textural contrast. Fresh herbs like mint and cilantro burst onto the scene, adding pops of color and a burst of invigorating fragrance. Chopped red onions or shallots offer a welcome crunch, and chilies, adjusted to your preference, deliver a pleasant warmth.

Laab's beauty lies in its adaptability and informality. The choice of protein allows for customization, catering to different dietary needs. Laab can be enjoyed as a main course with steamed sticky rice, offering a satisfying and well-rounded meal. However, it also thrives as part of a larger communal spread, alongside other Thai dishes like papaya salad or green curry. The vibrant flavors and invigorating elements of laab make it a perfect starter to awaken your taste buds and prepare you for the culinary adventure ahead.

Ingredients :

  • Ground Meat: Traditionally ground pork or chicken, but can be substituted with ground turkey, beef, or crumbled tofu for a vegetarian option (about 1 pound).
  • Fish Sauce: Provides a savory and umami depth (2 tablespoons).
  • Lime Juice: Adds a refreshing tang (2 tablespoons).
  • Toasted Rice Powder: A signature ingredient, offering a nutty aroma and textural contrast (3 tablespoons).
  • **(Optional)** Red Chili Flakes: Adjust the amount for your spice preference.
  • **(Optional)** Palm Sugar or Brown Sugar: A touch of sweetness to balance the flavors (1 tablespoon).
  • **(Optional)** Red Onion (minced): Adds a sharp and pungent flavor (1/4 cup).
  • Shallots (minced): A milder alternative to red onion (1/4 cup, about 2 shallots).
  • Garlic (minced): For an aromatic base (2 cloves).
  • Galangal (minced, optional): Offers a citrusy and floral note (1 inch piece).
  • Mint Leaves (chopped): Adds a refreshing and cooling flavor (1/2 cup).
  • Cilantro (chopped): Provides a bright and herbaceous taste (1/2 cup).

  • Fresh Lime Wedges: For squeezing extra lime juice over the laab (optional).
  • **(Optional)** Toasted Peanuts (chopped): For an extra textural contrast and a touch of richness (1/4 cup).

Instructions :

  • Cook the Protein: In a skillet or wok, heat a tablespoon of oil over medium heat. Add the ground meat and cook until browned and cooked through, breaking it up with a spoon as it cooks. Drain any excess fat.
  • Toast the Rice Powder (Optional): If not using pre-made toasted rice powder, heat a dry pan over medium heat. Add the raw rice and toast until golden brown, stirring constantly. Grind the toasted rice into a coarse powder using a mortar and pestle or a spice grinder.
  • Prepare the Aromatics and Herbs: Mince the garlic, galangal (if using), shallots, and fresh herbs (mint and cilantro).
  • Slice Red Onion (Optional): Thinly slice the red onion for garnish (optional).
  • Combine Ingredients: In a large bowl, combine the cooked ground meat, toasted rice powder, fish sauce, lime juice, red chili flakes (if using), and palm sugar (if using). Mix well to coat the meat evenly.
  • Add Aromatics and Herbs: Stir in the minced garlic, galangal (if using), shallots, mint, and cilantro. Toss to combine.
  • Taste and Adjust: Taste the laab and adjust the seasonings with additional fish sauce, lime juice, or chili flakes as needed.
  • Transfer to Plate: Transfer the laab to a serving plate.
  • Garnish (Optional): Garnish with sliced red onion, fresh lime wedges, and chopped peanuts (optional).
  • Serve with Sticky Rice: Serve the laab alongside steamed sticky rice for a complete meal.
Laab shines in its delightful presentation and contrasting textures. The vibrant mixture of ground meat, toasted rice powder, and fresh herbs forms a visually captivating mound. Pops of red from chilies (adjusted to your preference) peek through the green flecks of mint and cilantro. Sliced red onion (optional) adds a touch of color, while chopped peanuts (optional) offer a textural contrast. This dish is traditionally served with a plate of steamed sticky rice. The sticky rice acts as the perfect vehicle for scooping up the laab, ensuring you savor every flavorful bite. As you enjoy laab, the textures dance on your palate - the ground meat provides a satisfying chew, the toasted rice powder adds a delightful crunch, and the fresh herbs offer a refreshing coolness.

Laab is a versatile dish that can be enjoyed throughout the day. For a light and invigorating lunch, a serving of laab with sticky rice is a perfect choice. The fresh herbs like mint and cilantro provide a cooling and refreshing sensation, ideal for a warm midday meal. Laab can also be served as part of a larger communal spread for dinner. The bold flavors and distinct textures of laab act as a delightful starter, awakening your taste buds and preparing you for the culinary adventure ahead. Laab is not greasy or heavy, making it a great choice any time of day, especially when you crave a flavorful and satisfying meal that won't leave you feeling sluggish.
