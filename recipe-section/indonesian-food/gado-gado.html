How to Make Indonesian Gado - Gado

Gado-gado, a beloved Indonesian salad, is a vegetarian feast for the senses. Steamed or raw vegetables like green beans, cabbage, and bean sprouts create a colorful base, while hard-boiled eggs, fried tofu and tempeh add protein and heartiness. The crowning glory is a rich peanut sauce dressing, bringing a symphony of sweet, savory, and spicy flavors. Toasted shrimp crackers and fried shallots provide a delightful textural contrast, making gado-gado a satisfying main course or a flavorful side dish perfect for sharing at gatherings.

Ingredients :

  • Steamed vegetables (green beans, cabbage, carrot, bean sprouts)
  • Blanched vegetables ( chayote, kangkung (water spinach) - optional)
  • Hard-boiled eggs
  • Fried tofu (cut into squares or triangles)
  • Fried tempeh (cut into squares or triangles)
  • Peanut sauce dressing
  • Toasted shrimp crackers
  • Fried shallots (optional)
  • Boiled potatoes (optional)
  • Kerupuk (fish crackers) - optional (different from shrimp crackers)
  • Lotek (boiled green bean cake) - optional

Instructions :

  • Prepare the Vegetables: Wash and chop your chosen steamed vegetables (green beans, cabbage, carrots) into bite-sized pieces. Steam them until tender-crisp. You can also blanch vegetables like chayote or kangkung (water spinach) for a few minutes if using.
  • Hard-boil the Eggs: Place eggs in a pot, cover with water, and bring to a boil. Once boiling, remove from heat, cover, and let sit for 10-12 minutes. Peel and quarter the eggs.
  • Fry the Tofu and Tempeh: Cut tofu and tempeh into squares or triangles. Heat oil in a pan and fry them until golden brown and crispy. Drain on paper towels to remove excess oil.
  • Prepare the Peanut Sauce: Follow your preferred peanut sauce recipe and prepare it in advance.
  • Assemble the Gado-Gado: Arrange the steamed vegetables on a large serving platter. Top with hard-boiled egg quarters, fried tofu and tempeh, and any other optional ingredients like boiled potatoes or lotek.
  • Garnish and Serve: Sprinkle with fried shallots (optional) and toasted shrimp crackers. Serve the peanut sauce in a separate bowl for dipping, allowing everyone to customize their portion.
  • Enjoy! Toss everything together with the peanut sauce just before eating for a delightful combination of flavors and textures.
Gado-gado's versatility shines through in how you serve it. Traditionally, it's presented as a beautiful and vibrant centerpiece. Arrange the steamed vegetables on a large platter, creating a colorful base. Top it with the protein elements like fried tofu and tempeh, along with quartered hard-boiled eggs. This allows everyone to customize their plate , some might prefer a vegetarian option with just vegetables, while others can add protein. Don't forget the star of the show the peanut sauce! Serve it in a separate bowl for dipping, so people can adjust the amount to their taste preference. Finish the dish with a sprinkle of fried shallots for a delightful textural contrast and some toasted shrimp crackers on the side for a fun and satisfying crunch. Gado-gado is a perfect dish for any time of day. The light and refreshing nature of the steamed vegetables makes it ideal for a warm lunchtime meal. The protein and peanut sauce provide a satisfying feeling, keeping you energized throughout the afternoon. However, gado-gado also makes a fantastic light dinner option, especially on hotter days. The vegetables offer essential vitamins and minerals, while the peanut sauce adds healthy fats to keep you feeling full without feeling weighed down.
