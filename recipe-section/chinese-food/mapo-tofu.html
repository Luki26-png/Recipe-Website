How to Make Mapo Tofu

Mapo Tofu, a Sichuan classic, is a dish that tantalizes the taste buds with its fiery heat, savory depth, and surprising textural contrast. The star of the show is the silken tofu, cut into soft cubes and bathed in a rich and flavorful sauce. This sauce is where the magic happens. Fermented chili bean paste, also known as doubanjiang, forms the base, infusing the dish with its complex savory and spicy notes. Sichuan peppercorns add their unique numbing sensation, creating a delightful tingling effect on the tongue. Further depth comes from ingredients like Shaoxing wine, soy sauce, and a touch of sugar, creating a harmonious balance of flavors.

But Mapo Tofu is more than just a sauce and tofu. Often, ground pork is added for richness, but vegetarian versions can be equally satisfying with mushrooms or crumbled tempeh. Scallions, both white and green parts, add a pop of color and freshness, while a touch of cornstarch thickens the sauce to a luxurious consistency. The final flourish? A sprinkle of Sichuan peppercorns on top, a visual cue to the delightful numbing experience that awaits. Mapo Tofu is a dish that is both visually stunning and a flavor explosion, showcasing the unique and captivating character of Sichuan cuisine

Ingredients :

  • Soft Tofu: Silken tofu is preferred for its soft and smooth texture.
  • Doubanjiang (Fermented Chili Bean Paste): This is the key ingredient, providing the base flavor of the sauce.
  • Sichuan Peppercorns: Whole or ground peppercorns add the characteristic numbing sensation.
  • Garlic and Ginger: Essential aromatics for building flavor.
  • Shaoxing Wine (or Sherry): Adds complexity and a touch of sweetness.
  • Soy Sauce: Provides a savory base.
  • Sugar: Balances the spiciness.
  • **(Optional)** Chicken Broth or Water: Used to thin the sauce to the desired consistency.
  • Cornstarch or Potato Starch: Thickens the sauce and creates a glossy texture.
  • Ground Pork (optional): Adds richness to the dish. Vegetarian options can use crumbled tempeh or mushrooms.
  • Scallions: Chopped scallions, both white and green parts, add color and freshness.
  • Vegetable Oil: For cooking.
  • Cooked Rice: Served alongside the Mapo Tofu.

Instructions :

  • Drain and Cut the Tofu: Gently drain the tofu and cut it into bite-sized cubes. Be careful not to break the tofu too much.
  • Heat Oil: Heat vegetable oil in a wok or large pan over medium heat.
  • Stir-fry Aromatics: Add Sichuan peppercorns (whole or ground) and stir-fry for a few seconds until fragrant. Remove and set aside (optional, to garnish later).
  • Saute Aromatics: Add garlic and ginger to the hot oil and saute for a few seconds until fragrant.
  • Stir-fry Doubanjiang: Add the doubanjiang (fermented chili bean paste) and stir-fry for a minute, releasing its flavor and aroma.
  • Deglaze with Shaoxing Wine: Pour in Shaoxing wine (or sherry) and scrape up any browned bits from the bottom of the pan.
  • Add Soy Sauce and Sugar: Add soy sauce and sugar to the sauce and stir to combine.
  • **(Optional)** Thin the Sauce: If the sauce is too thick, add a little chicken broth or water to thin it to the desired consistency.
  • Thicken the Sauce: In a small bowl, whisk together cornstarch or potato starch with some water to create a slurry. Gradually add the slurry to the sauce while stirring constantly, until the sauce thickens slightly and becomes glossy.
  • Brown the Pork (optional): If using ground pork, add it to the pan and cook until browned, breaking it up with a spoon.
  • Add Tofu and Bring to a Simmer: Gently add the tofu cubes to the sauce and bring to a simmer. Be careful not to stir too vigorously to avoid breaking the tofu.
  • Garnish and Serve: Turn off the heat and stir in the reserved Sichuan peppercorns (if using) and chopped scallions (both white and green parts). Serve immediately over cooked rice.
